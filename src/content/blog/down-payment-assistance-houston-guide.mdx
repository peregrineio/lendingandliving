Here's something that surprises most first-time homebuyers I work with: **you don't need to save 20% for a down payment.** In fact, with the right programs, you might not need to save much at all.

Houston has some of the best down payment assistance (DPA) programs in Texas, and most buyers don't even know they exist. Today, I'm going to change that.

## The 20% Down Payment Myth

Let's start by busting the biggest myth in homebuying: you need 20% down to purchase a home.

The reality? The average first-time homebuyer puts down just 6-7%. And with down payment assistance programs, that number can drop even lower — sometimes to 0%.

Think about what that means. On a $300,000 home:
- **20% down** = $60,000 (what most people think they need)
- **6% down** = $18,000 (average first-time buyer)
- **With DPA** = $0-5,000 out of pocket (what's actually possible)

If the only thing keeping you from homeownership is the down payment, keep reading.

## What Is Down Payment Assistance?

Down payment assistance programs are grants, forgivable loans, and low-interest second mortgages designed to help homebuyers cover their down payment and closing costs. These programs are funded by:

- Federal government (through HUD)
- State of Texas
- Local counties and cities
- Nonprofit organizations

The best part? Many of these programs offer **forgivable loans** — meaning if you stay in the home for a certain number of years, you never have to pay the money back. It's essentially free money toward your home.

## Houston's Best DPA Programs

### 1. TDHCA My First Texas Home

The Texas Department of Housing and Community Affairs offers one of the most popular DPA programs in the state.

**Highlights:**
- Up to 5% of the loan amount for down payment/closing costs
- Available as a forgivable loan (forgiven after 3 years)
- Can be combined with FHA, VA, or USDA loans
- Available statewide

**Requirements:**
- First-time homebuyer (or haven't owned in 3 years)
- Meet income limits (vary by county and household size)
- Credit score of 620+
- Complete homebuyer education course

**For Houston (Harris County) 2026 Income Limits:**
| Household Size | Maximum Income |
|----------------|----------------|
| 1-2 persons | $97,300 |
| 3+ persons | $111,895 |

### 2. SETH 5 Star Texas Advantage Program

The Southeast Texas Housing Finance Corporation's 5 Star program is another excellent option.

**Highlights:**
- Up to 5% of loan amount
- Available as grant (no repayment required)
- Works with FHA, VA, USDA, and conventional loans
- No first-time buyer requirement

**Requirements:**
- Meet income and purchase price limits
- Credit score of 620+
- Primary residence only
- Complete homebuyer education

The SETH program is particularly valuable because you don't have to be a first-time buyer — even if you've owned a home before, you may qualify.

### 3. Harris County HAP (Homebuyer Assistance Program)

Harris County offers its own assistance program for residents.

**Highlights:**
- Up to $25,000 in assistance
- Forgivable over 5 years
- Covers down payment AND closing costs
- Additional assistance for certain professions (teachers, first responders)

**Requirements:**
- Must purchase in Harris County
- Income below 80% of area median income
- Credit score of 620+
- Complete HUD-approved counseling

### 4. City of Houston Homebuyer Assistance Program

If you're buying within Houston city limits, this program provides substantial assistance.

**Highlights:**
- Up to $40,000 in assistance (highest in the area)
- Forgivable over 5-10 years
- Can be combined with other programs
- Focus on underserved communities

**Requirements:**
- Purchase within Houston city limits
- Income at or below 80% AMI
- First-time homebuyer
- Complete homebuyer education

## Common DPA Myths — Debunked

### Myth 1: "DPA programs are too good to be true"

**Reality:** These programs are real and have helped thousands of Houston families become homeowners. They're funded by government entities with a mission to increase homeownership.

### Myth 2: "I make too much money to qualify"

**Reality:** Income limits are higher than you might think. A household of 3+ in Harris County can earn over $111,000 and still qualify for TDHCA programs.

### Myth 3: "The application process is too complicated"

**Reality:** When you work with an experienced loan officer (like me!), I handle the DPA application alongside your mortgage. It's one process, not two.

### Myth 4: "DPA programs have higher interest rates"

**Reality:** Most DPA programs have rates comparable to — or even better than — market rates. The rate depends on the program and your qualifications.

### Myth 5: "I have to pay it all back"

**Reality:** Many DPA programs are structured as forgivable loans. Stay in the home for 3-5 years, and the assistance is completely forgiven.

**Combined with DPA:**

You're a great candidate for down payment assistance if:

- Your household income is below the limits
