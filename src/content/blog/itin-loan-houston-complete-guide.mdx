## What Is an ITIN Loan?

An ITIN loan is a mortgage specifically designed for people who have an Individual Taxpayer Identification Number instead of a Social Security Number. The IRS issues ITINs to individuals who need to file taxes but aren't eligible for an SSN — including many hardworking immigrants who have been contributing to our economy for years.

Here's what makes ITIN loans special:

- **No Social Security Number required** — Your ITIN is all you need
- **Available for primary residences** — Buy the home you'll actually live in
- **Competitive interest rates** — While slightly higher than conventional loans, rates are reasonable
- **Traditional 30-year terms available** — Build equity over time

The key thing to understand is that ITIN loans are **not** the same as subprime loans from the past. These are legitimate mortgage products offered by reputable lenders who understand that taxpaying residents deserve the opportunity to own homes.

## Who Qualifies for an ITIN Loan in Houston?

You may qualify for an ITIN loan if you meet these basic requirements:

**Identification:**
- Valid ITIN number (not expired)
- Two forms of ID (passport, consular ID, driver's license)
- Proof of current address

**Financial Requirements:**
- Minimum credit score of 620-660 (varies by lender)
- At least 10-20% down payment
- Debt-to-income ratio below 43-50%
- Two years of tax returns filed with your ITIN

**Employment & Income:**
- Two years of stable employment history
- Consistent income documentation
- Can be W-2 employee, self-employed, or 1099 contractor

The good news? If you've been working, paying taxes, and managing your finances responsibly, you're likely closer to homeownership than you think.

## Documents You'll Need

Gathering the right documents is one of the most important steps in the ITIN loan process. Here's what you should start collecting:

**Identity Documents:**

- Texas driver's license or state ID

## What Interest Rates Can You Expect?

I'll be honest with you: ITIN loan interest rates are typically 1-2% higher than conventional mortgage rates. As of 2026, you might see rates in the 8-10% range, depending on your credit score, down payment, and other factors.

Why the higher rate? Lenders see ITIN loans as slightly higher risk because:

- There's no Social Security Number for traditional credit verification
- The borrower pool is smaller
- There are fewer secondary market options

However, here's what many people don't realize: **a higher interest rate today doesn't mean you're stuck with it forever.** Many of my ITIN clients refinance after 2-3 years of building credit and equity, often getting significantly lower rates.

The important thing is getting into a home and starting to build wealth through equity — not renting and building someone else's wealth.

## FAQ

**Q: Can I really buy a house without a Social Security number in Texas?**

Yes! Texas has no laws preventing ITIN holders from purchasing property. As long as you meet the lender's requirements and can make the payments, you can absolutely buy a home.

**Q: What if my ITIN is expired?**

You'll need to renew it before applying for a mortgage. The IRS renewal process takes 7-11 weeks, so start early. You can apply while waiting for renewal if you have proof that renewal is in progress.

**Q: Can my spouse use their SSN while I use my ITIN?**

Yes! Mixed-status couples can absolutely purchase homes together. In fact, having one borrower with an SSN can sometimes help with loan terms.

**Q: Do I need to be a US citizen to get a mortgage?**

No. You do not need to be a US citizen to purchase property in Texas. ITIN holders, visa holders, and permanent residents can all qualify for mortgages.

**Q: What if I have limited credit history?**

We can often work with alternative credit — things like rent payment history, utility payments, and phone bills. Having 12-24 months of on-time payments on these accounts can help establish creditworthiness.
